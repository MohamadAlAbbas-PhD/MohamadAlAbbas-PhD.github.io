Working Paper · Environmental Demography

Born in a Haze: Early-Life Exposure to Forest Fires and Child Health in Indonesia

NSF-PIRE Project · University of Pennsylvania

Examines the impact of forest fire–related haze on children's height-for-age (HAZ) and weight-for-age (WAZ) growth indicators using the Indonesia Family Life Survey (IFLS). Develops clustering algorithms and k-means quintile regression for exposure construction from satellite fire data. Links remotely sensed MODIS fire radiative power to geocoded child records across survey waves.

Who is Exposed and Who is Harmed? Social Disparities in Flood Exposure and Impact in Pernambuco, Brazil

Population & Environment (Accepted)

Examines divergence between satellite-derived flood inundation extent and self-reported flood exposure using household survey data in Pernambuco, Brazil. Explores the social stratification of measurement discordance: who under-reports, and why that matters for policy targeting. Employs logistic regression and spatial lag models.

Climate–Health across Biomes: Stratified Impacts of Precipitation and Temperature on Children's Health

Multi-country analysis · In progress

Links CHIRPS precipitation and NOAA temperature anomalies to diarrhea, fever, cough, and weight-for-height (WHZ) outcomes across multiple countries. Tests whether biome context, rather than administrative-unit fixed effects, moderates climate-health relationships, using interaction models and predictive margins.
